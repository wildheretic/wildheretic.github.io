# Tamarack mines #
{: .no_toc .bg-grey-lt-100 .px-2 .py-1 :} 

03 October 2022

Anybody who has ever looked into concave Earth theory (CET), will know about this experiment thanks to [Donald E. Simanek's article](/assets/pdfs/concave-earth/the-tamarack-mines-mystery.pdf) which appeared in the earlier days of the world wide web. In a nutshell, the experiments were these:

{: .highlight-title .mb-5 }
> Donald E. Simanek
> 
> In the fall of 1901 J.B. Watson, Chief Engineer at the Tamarack copper mine (S. of Calumet, Mich.) suspended 4250 foot long plumb lines down mine shafts. Measurements showed that the plumb lines were farther apart at the bottom than at the top, contrary to expectations.

**"Contrary to expectations"** is the understatement of our age. A hanging plumb line is at a precise right angle from the horizontal and shows a builder the true vertical for the place where he wants to build a wall. The true vertical always points to the center of gravity which means a plumb line also does the same.

We are supposed to live on the convex surface of a solid sphere so that plumb lines, in theory, should always point to the center of the Earth globe, which is supposed to be below our feet... except they didn't... at least the first experiments in 1901 did not. The lines hung in the Tamarack mines diverged in space instead.

<div style="text-align: center;"><figure ><img src="/assets/images/concave-earth/Tamarack-mines-centre-of-gravity.jpg" alt="Tamarack mines centre of gravity" ><figcaption class="figure-caption">Plumblines move apart in a concave earth as the center of gravity is above our heads not in the ground.</figcaption></figure></div>

The balls were expected to move closer together towards the center of the Earth because that is where the center of gravity should reside if it were a pull dependent on mass as Newton said it was. According to Newton, the larger the mass, the more attractive pull it possesses because its center of gravity is at the center of the mass.

This, by the way, has nothing to do with free-falling objects which fall in accordance with the inverse square laws only; whether it is a piano or tennis ball falling, both **apparently** fall at the same speed in a vacuum; although some people [claim otherwise](https://odysee.com/@ConcaveEarth:1/Gravity-Is-Not-What-We-Think-What-Are-They-Hiding_1080p:a).

Instead, in 1901, the balls moved further apart which would put the center of gravity in space, making it a push from outside, rather than a pull from within. So instead of gravity being a property of matter, it would be in actual fact a property of space or the ether. This is what concave earth theory predicts.

The experiments were reported in the newspapers at the time and also appeared in Professor McNair's paper, _Divergence of Long Plumb-Lines at the Tamarack Mine_ (Science, XV, 390 June 20, 1902) and the book _[Cellular Cosmonogy](/assets/pdfs/concave-earth/cellular-cosmogony.pdf)_ by Cyrus Teed and Ulysses Grant Morrow.

## Inital results ##
{: .toc } 

The first test in September 1901 used two no. 24 steel piano wires with 50 pound cast iron bobs hanging 4250 feet down **shaft 5**. Both bobs were also immersed in pails of engine oil to hinder undue vibrations. They were roughly 15 feet apart and created a divergence of 0.11 feet at the bottom, but were then moved slightly further apart to avoid obstacles and gave a **divergence** of 0.07 feet.

To rule out magnetism between the iron ventilation pipe running down the western side of the shaft and the plumb bobs, 50 pound lead balls were used and the test repeated, but this time the length of the wires was 120 feet shorter and situated in **shaft 2**. Again, a **divergence** of 0.10 feet was found.

## Repeat results ##
Just to be absolutely sure no magnetism was involved, the same experiments were repeated in January 1902 in shaft 4, but this time with bronze No. 20 piano wires which carried 60-pound lead bobs approximately 15 feet apart and 4,440 feet in length. They found a very slight **convergence** of 0.028 feet. Steel wires were used again alternating between the iron and lead bobs also giving similar converging results in **shaft 4**.

Lastly, the test was repeated in **shaft 5** with the bronze wire and lead bobs to give a bigger **diverging** reading than the 1901 test of 0.141.

## McNair's Interpretation ##
The consistent results within each of the different shafts led McNair to theorize that circulating air was the culprit with **shaft 5's** updraft along the western line causing the divergence. They managed to block off most of the updraft by moving the wire and sealing the top leaving only a very small circulating air current due to the hot air at the bottom of the shaft naturally moving up to the colder air at the top. This gave a very small **divergence** of 0.018 feet.

Interestingly, Morrow states that the 0.018 divergence in shaft 5, after the air current had been cut off, was nearer to the necessary divergence of a concave Earth. This divergence was considerably less and nearer the calculated divergence of "gravic rays" in the hollow globe, than that obtained when the air in the shaft was in circulation.

Shaft 2 had the same construction as shaft 5 and so was expected to have the same air current direction; and the western line in **shaft 4** was too close to the wall. This allowed for the circulating currents to push against it making them converge slightly. When this was rectified, the lines **diverged** by 0.04 feet.

So, when the air current variable had been largely rectified, the results were **a consistent divergence in all three shafts**.

## My interpretation ##
The air current theory sounds a reasonable conclusion, unlike Simanek's added opinion that the divergence was caused by a rotating Earth. As we know it is the heavens which rotate and not the Earth thanks to the experiments in the late nineteenth and early twentieth century.

Lastly, both Simanek and Mcnair agree that the gravity of the surrounding rock would be too negligible to affect the results. On this latter point, I might possibly disagree. They would be correct if it were the mass of the rock directly adjacent to the balls that would pull on the balls due to the traditional interpretation of the Cavendish experiment.

The Cavendish experiment shows that a suspended heavy object will be very slowly pulled towards a heavy material over a long period of time. It is assumed the heavy objects have enough mass to cause attraction via pull gravity. However, the push gravity interpretation says that it is the differential in gravity pressure between the two objects that causes the attraction.

Both objects block a very small bit more of the push gravity (or diamagnetic vortex) than the neighbouring air. This causes gravity to flow faster downwards in the air, thereby creating slightly less pressure in gravity between the two objects which pulls them both together. This is [Bernoulli's Principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bernoulli%27s_principle) for gravity rather than air. This principle is said to cause airplanes to lift off the ground.

The Tamarack mine shafts have 4250 feet of ground to block push gravity. This is a considerable amount. It should have a fairly significant effect on suspended objects close to it that deep in the shaft. Would it be enough to pull a vertically suspended ball in a drum of oil? Possibly. A probable effect of this was shown in different mine shafts using free-falling objects.

### Free-falling objects down mine shafts

The following was reported from a [newspaper clipping](http://concaveearth.blogspot.com/p/blog-page.html), found in box 13 of the James Fisher collection at the Michigan Tech archives and Copper Country Historical Collection. A very similar article appears in the Detroit Free Press, August 20; but only one of the steel balls (“marbles”) was found, and it was 800 feet below the surface, not 500 feet.

{: .highlight-title .mb-5 }
> Newspaper article
> 
> It is an interesting fact, and one not generally known that nothing that falls from the mouth of the deepest mining shaft in the world ever reaches the bottom. This has been demonstrated at the famous Red Jacket shaft of the Big Calumet and Hecla copper mine at Calumet.
> 
> The article, no matter what shape or size it may be, is invariably found clinging to the east side of the shaft. One day a monkey wrench was dropped, but it did not get to the bottom. It was found lodged against the east side of the shaft several hundred feet down.
> 
> This incident coming to the attention of the Michigan College of Mines, it was decided to make a careful test of the apparent phenomenon. It was decided best to use a small but heavy spherical body, and a marble, tied to a thread, was suspended about twelve feet below the mouth of the shaft.
> 
> When the marble was absolutely still, assuring that it would drop straight, the thread was burned through by the flame of a candle. The marble fell, but at a point 500 feet (800 feet?) from the surface brought up against the east wall of the shaft.

Whether the east wall has any significance is unknown. I would assume, if the object had been dropped nearer to the west wall, it would have found itself lodged there. We will never know for sure though. More experimentation is needed. However, on the face of it, this phenomenon not only helps verify the Cavendish experiment, but it also negates its pull gravity interpretation.

As McNair and modern sketic [Donald Simanek](/assets/pdfs/concave-earth/the-tamarack-mines-mystery.pdf) of Lockhaven University both point out, gravity would be too neglible to effect an object, especially over such a short period of time when free-falling. Not so the push interpretaton, as 4250 feet of rock has a much stronger gravitational attraction via Bernoulli's Principle than it would if gravity were a pull from a local adjacent mass.

However, another unverified experiment at Tamarack Mines was carried out which could negate this push gravity intepretaton variable when it comes to suspended balls.

## Another experiment ##
Sadly, neither the newspapers, nor Mcnair's paper mention a crucial additional experiment which could rule out the free-falling Cavendish effect. This was only fully reported in the November 1960 edition of _[Flying Saucers, The Magazine of Space Conquest](/assets/pdfs/concave-earth/experiments-with-bobs-inside-earth-theory-free-energy-cosmos-satellite-moon-planets-astronomy-cellular-cosmogony-astrophysics.pdf)_ written by Ray Palmer, and partly in the book _Cellular Cosmonogy_.

<div style="text-align: center;"><figure><img src="/assets/images/concave-earth/palmer-ray.jpg" alt="Ray Palmer" ><figcaption class="figure-caption">Ray Palmer</figcaption></figure></div>

Palmer claimed that there was a **8.22 inches divergence (0.685 feet)** between one plumb line which was hung in shaft 2 and another in shaft 5, both 4250 feet apart and deep, and with a **4250 long** transverse tunnel connecting the two at the bottom. The engineers used this figure to calculate the distance of the center of gravity by following this angle of divergence further upwards, which was apparently found to be around **4000 miles up in space** (not in the ground).

{: .highlight-title .mb-5 }
> Ray Palmer
> 
> It did not take the Tamarack engineer long to discover the divergence that would be necessary to complete a 360 spherical circumference. There was only one difficulty as expressed be the plumb lines, it would be the circumference of the inside of a sphere, and not the outside; Further, the center of gravity, as expressed by the angles formed by the plumb lines, would be approximately 4,000 miles out in space! 
> 
> Obviously this could not be true, because if the Chinese were to make calculations based on a similar pair of mine shafts in their country, on the opposite side of the globe, the center of gravity would be found to be 4,000 miles in the other direction. The center of gravity, according to the plumb lines, was a sphere's surface, some 16,000 miles in diameter. Any place, 4,000 miles up, was the center of gravity.

If this were true, you may think well, maybe the Earth is convex but the entire circumference 4000 miles up is the center of gravity, as if the Earth is encased in a ball putting increasing pressure down on it? Except the center of gravity is just that... the center. All lines on any place converge on ONE POINT, not a continuous plate.

The diameter of the earth is said to be [3,963 miles (6,378km)](https://www.space.com/17638-how-big-is-earth.html). Therefore, there is only one conclusion from Palmer's citation, which is the Earth is concave and we live on the inside.

Also, it is assumed that each plumb line was suspended vertically from the **center** of each mine shaft. This would help rule out the Cavendish effect on the ball as the reduction in gravity pressure would initially be equal all around it.

The biggest problem with Palmer's account is the lack of detail. It is relatively easy to accurately measure the distance between two vertical lines at the top and bottom when they are in one shaft. It is another thing altogether to accurately measure thousands of feet between two shafts.

Did they use an already measured horizontal line or wire that was pulled fairly taut between the two shafts? The difference between the bottom and top was to be measured only in inches. Not much room for error when thousands of feet are to be initially measured out.

However, Ray Palmer wasn't the only one to mention this additional experiment. A more contemporary source at the time was Ulysses Grant Morrow, a geodetist (Earth surveyor) and member of the Koreshan Unity whose members believed the Earth to be concave.

In the book Cellular Cosmogony, written by both Ulysses Morrow and Cyrus Teed, the results of this experiment were unknown to Morrow because it seems they were being carried out at the time of writing. Morrow also states that the two shafts were 3,200 feet apart instead of Palmer's 4,250 feet. Nevertheless, he claimed to confidently predict the divergence would be 8.22 inches. On [page 201](/assets/pdfs/concave-earth/cellular-cosmogony.pdf):

{: .highlight-title .mb-5 }
> Cellular Cosmogony (page 201)
> 
> The distance between shafts no.2 and no.5 is 3,200 feet. It was the intention of the mining engineer to have the twenty-ninth level opened between the two shafts, a line suspended in each shaft, and measurements taken at the top and bottom.
> 
> The calculated downward divergence of two perpendiculars 3,200 apart is 8.22 inches for the length of 4,250 feet; and we declare with confidence and certainty, that the two plumb-lines in the proposed experiment just outlined, will approximate this divergence.

Morrow and Teed were highly religious folk who were not the sort of people to deliberately lie or mislead. They were also unlikely to be mistaken as their geodetic experiment (the Rectilineator experiment) was nothing but pedantic in its precision.

It could be that they themselves had been misinformed of such an experiment, or perhaps the test had been scheduled to take place but was abandoned. Another possibility is that this experiment did indeed occur, but the results were too controversial to be published - a mini-conspiracy of sorts. Whatever the truth, we will probably never know.

## Conclusion ##
For Tamarack mines to conclusively prove that the earth is concave, Ray Palmer's account and the result of the latter experiment would have to be conclusively true. There is possible doubt in this regard, despite the geodeists Teed and Morrow at least verifying that same experiment as a proposal. We are also lacking much needed detail as to how the experiment was carried out, if it took place at all.

Professor McNair's account of the initial experiments is testimony enough. However, the push gravity interpretation of the Cavendish experiment throws a spanner in the works. A fool-proof repeat of the last experiment at two deep and connected mine shafts is needed to verify the shape of the earth.
